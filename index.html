CORRUPTION AND FRAUD: ZERO-TOLERANCE

ENGIE prohibits all forms of corruption and fraud, regardless of their nature, timing, location, circumstances, or amount.

No form of corruption or influence peddling, no matter how minor, will be tolerated.

This includes payments made to a public authority to expedite an action, involvement in political financing, issuing unjustified invoices, professional opportunities (including internships) outside HR procedures.

The fight against fraud also includes refraining from forging or falsifying a document.

RESPECT FOR HUMAN RIGHTS

This is why the Group is committed to conducting its business in compliance with internationally recognised human rights wherever it operates.

This involves: A respectful and inclusive work environment

Combatting violence, moral or sexual harassment, and sexist behaviour

Refusal of forced labour and child labour

Respect for freedom of association

Respect for the rights of local communities

COMMITMENT TO ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION

ENGIE attaches particular importance to the environment, its preservation and sustainable development.

Our Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) policy aims to place the environment at the heart of our concerns.

The Group’s ambition is to make energy and services a source of progress and harmonious development.

ENGIE ensures that its partners, suppliers, and clients adhere to social and environmental practices in line with its commitments.

The Group uses the most appropriate methods and techniques to promote sustainable development.

Research and innovation are encouraged to develop know-how in the fields of quality and safety, materials recovery and recycling, saving natural resources and reducing pollution.

COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS AND REGULATIONS

At all times and in all places, ENGIE complies with applicable laws and regulations.

This involves: Compliance with international sanctions and export control regulations

Fair commercial practices and competition

Protection of personal data, in compliance with laws and regulations including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and national legislations

Protection of the company and its assets. This covers the following aspects:

  • The protection of information
  • Compliance with rules on insider information and insider trading
  • Appropriate use of the company’s digital tools
  • Protecting and respecting intellectual property
  • Protecting company assets
  • Communication with third parties: investors, analysts, media, social networks

Research and innovation are encouraged to develop know-how in the fields of quality and safety, materials recovery and recycling, saving natural resources and reducing pollution.

INTEGRITY AND LOYALTY IN OUR RELATIONSHIPS WITH THIRD PARTIES

ENGIE attaches the highest importance to integrity and loyalty in its interactions with third parties, and particularly with public authorities.

This involves: Heightened vigilance when working with business consultants and certain intermediaries (exceptional use only)

Transparency with public authorities

A policy of solidarity through patronages, sponsorships and partnerships
